Space Mining

A rudimentary arcade game simulating human mining efforts in our solar system.

How did you envision your controller in the beginning and how did that vision change?

My original intentions was to simply remap the controls from a mouse and keyboard to a button and joystick or, perhaps, a pair of potentiometers. While that vision has largely remained the same, the final system mechanisms did change to introduce challenge and chance into an otherwise straightforward simulation.

How did the physical peculiarities of the sensors influence your interaction?

It really was quite simple. The joystick is a natural fit for two-dimensional movement and it was easy to make the transition from mouse controls to the new affordance. The button was a bit more complicated. The previous system necessitated the use of several keys, but for an arcade control scheme the simpler the better. Therefore, I had to reduce the amount of buttons down to one if any at all. The current targeting mechanic is a direct result of reducing down the necessary inputs.

How did the communication schema influence your interaction?

While I originally had only basic markings to provide subtle suggestions to how the control mapping worked, ultimately, there's only two methods of input. After some user testing, I found that introducing some illustrations on the controller helped people immerse themselves into the game, but there was never any confusion regarding the controls.

How did you settle on the physical shape of your controllers?

While I did play around with the idea of a handheld controller, ultimately I thought that this system would make the most sense on an arcade control panel. That, and a tighter controller wouldn't have been able to accomodate the M-F wires that connected the input hardware to the breadboard. There certainly is room for streamlining it, but it'd be difficult in the breadboards current configuration.

What would be some paths for future development of the project?

There are some performance issues in the current build that I'd like to sort out, in addition to various improvements to graphics, and increasing the build quality of the controller.
